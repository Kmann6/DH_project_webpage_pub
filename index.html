Project Overview

This project collects product details and project descriptions of consumer products that use historical narratives or historical figures and events to teach consumers about history in The United States. While the time frame of interest for this project in the 20th century, all submissions are welcome to create as comprehensive a database as possible.

WWII greatly impacted the U.S.'s manufacturering capabilities and new technologies made consumer products less expensive and more accessible for many. This, combined with reduced demand for war materials, helped foster a burgeoning leisure consumer society.

Research Question

With new tools at America's disposal, this project aims to demonstrate how historical narratives were used to create engaging and educational consumer products and asks: what narratives, events, or figures were most commonly privileged?

Why It Matters

People learn about their world and environment in many different ways. Formal education is well-documented and important research is being done on how we have shaped pedagogy in the U.S. However, it is equally important to understand the role of popular culture and public memory in shaping historical understanding.
