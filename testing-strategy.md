# Testing Protocol 

**Intended visitor**
This site is intended for use by distinct user audiences. In the first, this site is intended for the author herself to further her research and test crowdsourcing options.The principle contributing audience is hobbyists to fulfill the crowdsourcing element of the research project and provide this group with a database to which they can contribute and find details of other collectibles. Third, this website is intended for historical and social science research which shaped the product details recorded in the database itself. 

**Site intention**
This website is intended for users to clearly understand the purpose and  use for which the database is being compiled and to encourage visitors to add their own memory of specific products - now considered collectibles. 

**Initial testing**
Prior to peer review, this website was view on multiple devices and sent to three nonacadmeic viewers to ensure language, data table, and navigation were accessible to nonacedmic viewers (given the crowdsourcing page). These testers were aged 74, 45, and 16.

**Peer Reviewed Testing Protocol**
The website link was sent to two reviewers without additional context. Reviewers were asked to answer the below questions: 

1. Can you restate the research question after a brief scan of the landing page? 
2. Is the dataset on the findings page easy to review and column titles clear?
3. Is the call to action (Add Your Story) clear from the home page?
4. Is the form on "Add Your Story" easy to scroll through and questions easy to read?
5. Is it clear where the repository is located on the home page? 
6. Is it clear that this research site is one part crowdsourcing and one part narrative analysis?

**General Question**
Since the crowdsourcing form is not atually live to test currently, I am most interested in whether users understand the purpose of the project and understand the claims made about historical learning.


**User Responses**

Feedback from Tester A

1. Can you restate the research question after a brief scan of the landing page? 

Yes, the research question is clearly stated on the homepage and is also apparent in the project overview. This section provides a clear sense of the research framework and an understanding of the research aim.

2. Is the dataset on the findings page easy to review and column titles clear?

Yes, the dataset is clearly displayed on the findings page, and the column titles are mostly understandable. One column was just interesting, called “type_name,” because it contains the same data as the “product_name” column. In this regard, short explanations would be better for presenting the dataset more explicitly. 

3. Is the call to action (Add Your Story) clear from the home page?

Unfortunately, it was not easy to navigate from the homepage, but when I landed on the “Add Your Story” page, it became clearer and more understandable what the aim is.


4. Is the form on “Add Your Story” easy to scroll through and questions easy to read?

Yes, it is easy to scroll and choose which product the user wants to add. Specifically, the product type and type of history facilitate the user by providing predetermined options that can be chosen easily. In the future, the options may be expanded by adding more product types and types of history. In my opinion, the best section was sharing a story or memory of a particular product, which may increase crowdsourced engagement in the future.

5. Is it clear where the repository is located on the home page? 

Yes, it is apparent at the end of the homepage, along with information about the researcher and the institution.

6. Is it clear that this research site is one part crowdsourcing and one part narrative analysis?

As a graduate student, I understand the research methods and approach. However, someone who may not be accustomed to these kinds of projects may take time to understand the methodology. Therefore, it can be useful to place them under the Project Overview to increase accessibility for the general audience and to showcase the project’s main framework on the homepage.
